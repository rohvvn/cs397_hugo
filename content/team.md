---
title: "Pittsburgh Steelers"
summary: "History, identity, and key facts about the Steelers."
---

The Pittsburgh Steelers are one of the NFL’s most storied franchises. Founded in 1933, the team is known for its hard-nosed identity, passionate fanbase, and a legacy built on dominant defense and clutch performances.

Key facts:

- Founded: 1933 (originally as the Pittsburgh Pirates)
- Super Bowl Championships: 6 (IX, X, XIII, XIV, XL, XLIII)
- Conference: AFC | Division: North
- Home Stadium: Acrisure Stadium (North Shore, Pittsburgh)
- Team Colors: Black and Gold
- Head Coach: Mike Tomlin (since 2007)

Signature identity:

- The “Steel Curtain” defense defined the 1970s dynasty.
- Physical, disciplined play on both sides of the ball.
- Loyal Terrible Towel–waving fans at home and on the road.

Notable rivalries:

- Baltimore Ravens — modern, physical matchups with playoff stakes.
- Cleveland Browns — historic Rust Belt rivalry.
- Cincinnati Bengals — intense divisional battles.

Culture and legacy:

Pittsburgh’s blue-collar roots are woven into the team’s ethos. Generations of Steelers football have produced Hall of Fame legends and unforgettable moments—from the Immaculate Reception to dominant championship runs.
